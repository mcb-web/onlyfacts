Product Vision: Project "Factual News"

1. The Problem
Current Dutch news media (NOS, Telegraaf, Volkskrant, etc.) is increasingly fragmented and polarized. Readers are trapped in information bubbles, consuming news through a "sensationalist lens" or specific political biases. Reading multiple sources to find the truth is exhausting and time-consuming, leading to news-fatigue, stress, and societal division.

2. Our Mission
To create a radically objective news platform that restores trust through transparency. We don't tell users what to think; we provide them with the raw, distilled facts and show them how others are framing the story.

3. Core Principles
Neutrality by Design: No adjectives, no emotional triggers, no clickbait. If it's not a fact, it's not in the lead.

Radical Transparency: Every synthesized article must clearly show its homework. If we say "The Right is reporting this," we link directly to the source.

Cognitive Calm: The UI should be clean, minimalist, and "slow." No flashing breaking news banners or anxiety-inducing red colors.

Multi-Perspective Literacy: We don't hide bias; we map it. We help users understand the "why" behind the different political angles.

4. The User Experience
A user visits at 7:00 AM. They see one clear, factual headline about a major event. They immediately see that NRC and Trouw are covering it from a social perspective, while De Telegraaf focuses on the economic impact. The user leaves the site feeling informed and calm, rather than outraged.

5. Success Metrics
Bias-to-Fact Ratio: How effectively can we strip emotional language from 10 different sources into 1 objective report?

Political Coverage: Ensuring a balanced representation of the Dutch political spectrum and Tweede Kamer voting data.
